At 200 × 150 cm, it meets you first as a wall of accumulated gesture — thick black strokes, layered paint, a topography of marks that suggests something between a graffiti wall and a palimpsest. Then the eye catches it: a single CAPTCHA character, warped, oblique, intact. It floats in the density like a message from another system.

[Benka](/artists/benka) works with the tension between digital authentication and physical mark-making. The CAPTCHA — that familiar anti-bot test designed to prove you are human — becomes, in his hands, a fractured almost illegible sign. A former clinical psychologist who researched smartphone addiction, he brings an unusual conceptual depth to abstraction. His work questions what happens when the mechanism that proves our humanity becomes the subject of art.

This piece belongs to his ongoing CAPTCHA series, begun in 2015. [Benka](/artists/benka) now exhibits across Europe and the US.
