Among the works in this series, this is the one that most openly courts the horizon. The format itself — 200 × 120 cm, emphatically wide — invites a reading that is almost landscape. And the composition obliges: a dense rain-dark navy at the base, a luminous white atmosphere above, and between them, a series of electric pink gestures that cross the surface like something half-remembered.

The pink does not land. It sweeps, arcs, disperses. This is the point — Cheramy working with impermanence not as subject matter but as formal strategy. Each technique visible here has a different cadence: spray is instantaneous, brush builds, drip waits. The surface contains several speeds at once.

For a dedicated wall, this is a canvas that generates atmosphere. The deep navy anchors the room; the pink gesture provides the animation.
