# "WABI SABI 23092023" by Sébastien Cheramy — Mixed Media on Canvas, 2023

---

It operates on a single sustained contradiction. The upper field exhales — pale blues, drifting whites, a quiet that reads almost meteorological. Then the lower register erupts: hot pink, deep charcoal, acid yellow, marks that refuse to stay still. The two halves should not cohere. Yet they do.

Cheramy works within a practice shaped by graffiti, calligraphy, and abstract expressionism — registers that are, each in their own way, about the pressure of a mark made in real time, under physical commitment. The title invokes wabi-sabi, the Japanese aesthetic that finds beauty in impermanence. He does not illustrate the idea. He enacts it.

At 150 × 160 cm, it commands a wall without overwhelming a room. It holds differently at distance — the atmospheric upper half taking over — than up close, where the texture and layering of the lower zone become a separate conversation.
